What is Counter-Strike?

Counter-Strike: Global offensive(CS:GO or just CS) is a 5 vs 5 objective based first person shooter(FPS).

CS:GO was released August 12th, 2012 so this year is the 10th anniversry. However, Counter-Strike as a franchise has been around since 1999, so it has been as massive force and influence on the industry for as long as I have been alive.

Compared to some of its contemporaries, CS is smaller, more constrained, and simple. Simultaneously, it is a game with infinite depth and replayability.

Counter-Strike's gameplay loop is easy to watch, but very difficult to master. This makes it perfectly suited for Esports, which is why it has remained a dominant and heavily popular game in that scene.

It is a game I have close to 3,000 hours in, and I don't see myself stopping anytime soon. I have yet to find another FPS that can replace the way CS:GO makes me feel. I hope that after viewing this site, you too can understand the passion and love I still have for this rich game.

History

This is a loose path from the very beginning to the current iteration

Half Life Mod

Counter-strike does not start with the first game in the series. It acutally starts with a game called Half Life released in 1998 by Valve. You play as Gordan Freeman, a scientist who needs to escape the Black Mesa Research Facility after it is invaded by aliens. You explore, solve puzzles, and fight aliens and the military trying to contain the event. This game is considered a master peice, maintaining a 96% positive rating on the game over two decades later. Fans of the game created a mod of Half Life that used the source engine that powered the single player game to create a multiplayer experience. This mod was so popular that Counter-Strike became a game.
This aspect of its creation is part of what makes CS so unique. CS is a game that came from the community, and the community is the heart of the game

Counter Strike 1.6

Obviously, the first release was version 1.0, but when referring to this game now, 1.6 is the ubiquitous term. This was the first official game released and the game that many Counter Strike purists look back on as the best entry in the franchise. It has the classic maps and was very heavily focused on the community just having fun. I was 1 years old when this game was released in 2000, so I never played it, but the influence of this game can be seen in other aspects such as meme culture. The radio commands from this game are goofy and wonderful.

Counter Strike: Source

Counter Strike: Source (CS:S) was the next major update in 2004. CS: Conditon Zero (CS:CZ) is described as very similar to 1.6 but with content updates like adding bots and a single player campaign. We are skipping it since most players didn't stop play 1.6 for CS:CZ. Source is somewhat divisive and nowadays is also played much less than 1.6. AVG player counts for Source are aroud 3.5k while 1.6 has about 10k players each month.
However, Source is important because it was a massive update that dramatically changed the feel of Counter Strike. The physiscs were changed for player movement, grenades, and the graphics received a massive overhaul. I actually got this game to play garry's mod and only use it for surfing occaisonally.

Counter Strike: Global Offensive

In 2012, Valve Finally got around to the next major Counter Strike update: Counter Strike: Global Offensive(CS:GO) The graphics received a massive overhaul to make the game look much more presentable than an obviously early 2000s game. Graphics aren't everything, but this game was the newest when I first started getting into PC gaming around 2010-2011 for games like Starcraft 2 and Minecraft. CS:GO has seen many updates in the past ten years, and in many ways is a much different game than launch, some good some bad. CS:GO is the game official supported by Valve and is the platform that is used for competition among the best players in the world. It is a game I usually play at least once a week, despite the fact that I am washed up.

Why I love this game

Gameplay

The gameplay for Counter Strike is simply unparralled by other FPS games. CS:GO has so much depth to it. Winning a match is so much deeper than having the highest amount of points, or clicking faster than others.

    These are all the pieces that make up the gameplay:

  1. Map Knowledge
    • Knowing all of the spots players may or can be
    • Knowing the proper utility(smokes, flashbangs, HE grenade, firebombs) for each map
    • General familiarity with everything
  2. Game Sense
    • Making inferences on where the other players probably are
    • Making inferences on which angles enemy players are holding
    • Utilizing that information to outplay your opponent
  3. Teamwork
    • Coordinating with your team to perform successful executes and/or rotations
    • Communication(i.e. I heard footsteps apartments)
    • managing your teams economy in sync to ensure best chance of success
  4. Raw skill
    • Proper crosshair placement
    • Proper spray control
    • Reaction time

How Do you play Counter Strike?

The basics

This could take all day, so I'm going to keep it as simple and constrained as possible. Many skills just come from playing the game for thousands of hours, but these are important for newbies

    The Competitive Bomb plant/defuse Rule Set

  • 5 vs 5
  • Each round is 2 minutes long plus bomb timer if planted(add 45 seconds)
  • You need to buy weapons, armor, and defuse kit if CT(Don't be a loser, buy a defuser)
    • Loser takes 10 seconds to defuse
    • Defuser takes 5 seconds to defuse
  • Most maps are set up in three lane style(bomb site a and b, and mid)
  • Team damage is on

    Movement

  • Firing your weapon while moving or middair causes inaccuracy
    • Hold A+D at the same time to instantly stand still before firing
    • Actively Think about coming to complete stop before shooting
  • Crouching mid jump will let you jump on taller objects
  • Source engine gives a lot of control mid air
    • Holding A or D while in midair plus mouse movement lets you strafe which allows you to accelerate your velocity
    • Avoid pressing W or S mid air
    • Surf servers or Jump maps can help you improve your movement

    Weapons

  • Knives
    • You Spawn with this
    • High kill bounty($1500)
    • Right click is Heavy attack
      • Does 55 Damage to amrored opponents and 65 to unarmoured
      • Second swing is slow
      • Will insta kill if baackstab
    • Left click is light attack
      • If you miss the first attack, the next swing will do less damage
      • 34/21 to armored and 40/25 to unamored
      • takes two backstabs to finish
    • Best combo is left + left + right in melee
  • Pistols
    • You spawn with one
    • High bounties
    • cheap and good for save/eco rounds
    • Avoid longe range unless deagle
  • Sub Machine Guns
    • Relatively inexpensive
    • Good for force or light buy
    • Good close range
  • Shotguns
    • Relatively inexpensive
    • Good for anti eco(enemy team is saving)
    • High kill bounties
  • Assualt Rifles
    • Medium Price
    • Should be your bread and butter
    • Both M4 and AK are all around great weapons
  • Snipers
    • AWP is 1 shot kill above waist
    • Really expensive
    • Low kill reward
    • beware of losing this
  • Heavy Machine Guns
    • Don't buy m249
    • Negev is broken(really good ct)

    Utility

    BUY UTILITY WHENEVER POSSIBLE!

  • Smoke
    • lasts 15 seconds
    • Good for getting better angles
    • Good at preventing a push(person watching smoke has advantage)
  • Flashbang
    • Blinds enemies or allies
    • Good to throw right before poushing angle
    • Good against AWP
  • Firebomb
    • Lasts 7 seconds
    • Good at flushing a player out of a corner
    • Good at preventing a push
  • HE grenade
    • Good at finishing wounded enemies
    • Two nades together can finish a player
    • Nade stacking is decent eco strategy

    Firing your weapon

  • All weapons are accurate when standing still
  • All weapons have unique spray patterns that are almost identical
  • All weapons have random spread
  • Open an aim trainer from steam workshop to practice aim

    Economy

  • Max cash is 16,000
  • If a teammate can't afford utility, armor, and a weapon but you can; you should buy them
  • Ask for a buy if you can't afford everything
  • Save if everyone is broke
  • Make sure your teams economy is synchronized. Buy when they buy and save when they save
  • Buy every round squad is bad

This is just the tip of the iceburg, but good information to know before you start playing. And remember to have fun!

Alternatives

The comp mode that comes with CSGO is actually the casual mode because they have 64tick servers, terrible anti-cheat, and less serious players.

To address these concerns, private companies were formed to provide a true competitive experience.

  1. Faceit
    • 128tick servers
    • better anti-cheat
    • Tournaments
    • "Toxicity Prevention system"
  2. ESEA
    • 128tick servers
    • better anti-cheat
    • Cash prizes for high level players
    • Organized league structure
