Website Evaluations

Gatesnfences.com

gatesnfences.com is a website for selling and installing fences in residential areas in Florida. Virtually any gate type.

  • Contrast: All the text is readable. On the menu on the left side of the screen they outlined the menu items but some of the items are too long and go outside the borders. On The borders, there is white on light gray text making it difficult to read. The colors in general are all over the place too.
  • Repetition: The pages are consistent, but within each page, there is little repetion between where headers go, what colors are links, or in that menu, some have different outlines which make it look like a heading, but they aren't sometimes. Some of the picture change when you hober them, and some don't.
  • Alignment: The alignment on this page is all over the place. The page seems contained, but, the search bar on the right sticks out. It feels like everything was thrown on the page. Some items are just off center, like the 'Dealers Application' at the bottom.
  • Proximity: Most of the text boxes lacks a header or an easy way to identify what is even being talked about without reading the whole thing. Looking back, the header might actually be to the left of the text so I was confused. I can't tell.

Slack.com

Slack is googles take on a team communication service. It is for everyone, but I think it is more for the business market than discord for example.

  • Contrast: All the text is very easy to read. The headings are obvious and the buttons use distinct colors. Different macro sections use different background colors to make it more obvious.
  • Repetition: Throughout the page everything is formatted consistently. Header, followed by subtext, with links underneath and picutres wrapping the text on either side.
  • Alignment: Everything is in alignemnt on the page with nothing sticking out awkwardly.
  • Proximity: It is extremely obvious what belongs with what because only relevant information is grouped together. Pictures relate directly to text around them. The subtext relates directly to the heading it is under.
